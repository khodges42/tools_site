Palimpsest Wound

A page is trying to remember every sentence ever written on it. Scrape away the returning ink only where the reading line needs passage. Every correction bruises the paper and teaches the stain where to come back.

Rules of damaged paper

The repeated action is subtraction. You do not move a character or solve a prepared target; you decide where not to touch.

  • Drag or hold on the page to scrape ink away.
  • The black reading line descends. Keep the band around it mostly clear.
  • Scraping creates pale bruises. Bruised paper attracts new ink later.
  • Gentle scraping scars less. Hard scraping clears fast but wakes old text.
  • G/H switch tools. R restarts.
First thirty seconds: wait half a breath. Let the reading line show what actually matters, then scrape a narrow corridor instead of cleaning the whole page.
A static browser game about restraint, accumulation, and irreversible cleanup. The loop is observe encroaching memory → remove only enough → inherit the scars you made.
