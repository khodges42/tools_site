Linotype Wake

Set a small-town obituary on a hot-metal linecaster. Type the copy, but do not hammer one side of the magazine until the matrices swell. Wait to cool, scrape burrs with backspace, cast clean lines with space.

Foreman notes

Use your real keyboard, or the buttons. This is not a speed test. It is a machine sympathy test.

  • Letters on the left half heat the left magazine; right-half letters heat the right.
  • Alternating sides lets brass cool. Mashing one side makes burrs.
  • Backspace scrapes one burr, but costs time and cools unevenly.
  • Space casts the current line only at a word break.
First thirty seconds: type the highlighted obituary copy. Pause when one magazine climbs. The machine rewards cadence, not panic.
A static typing-machine game. No avatar, grid, cards, collection route, tuning dials, or item placement. The loop is physical cadence: compose copy while balancing two overheating magazines in an obsolete linecaster.
