Rules of late brass

The repeated action is phase interference. You are not steering a body, placing pieces, matching a target, or cleaning a surface. You move a temporary patch of slow time and then inherit the payback.

  • Drag, touch, or move the pointer to position the hush.
  • Hold/click/touch to open the hush. Hands inside it slow down.
  • When a hand leaves, stored lateness becomes a speed burst.
  • Score when exactly one hand crosses the blue gate.
  • If two or more hands meet in the red gate, the clock records an agreement. Seven agreements breaks the watch.
  • Space opens the hush. 1/2 switch width. R restarts.
First thirty seconds: do not hold the hush on the red gate. Tap briefly just before a hand arrives, so one hand becomes late while the others pass.
A static browser clock-phase game. No folding stack, no tide equalization, no projection coverage, no erasure/regrowth, no flock disturbance, no occupational loop. The loop is read phase → delay one moving hand → survive the debt when time catches up.
