Rules of borrowed tide

The repeated action is global persuasion. You never steer a body or imitate a target; one pull changes every channel at once.

  • Drag anywhere to set the moon-pull direction and strength.
  • Water flows through channels from high pools to low pools, biased by the pull.
  • Tap a channel to pinch it briefly. Pinched channels move slowly, then reopen.
  • Hold all pool levels close together for four seconds to seal the treaty.
  • Dry or flooded pools grow salt. Too much salt ends the shore.
First thirty seconds: drag gently sideways, then reverse before the end pools flood. Tap one loud channel to slow the overshoot.
