## Playable content added

Replaced the Chapter 7 placeholder with a playable 1849 Roman Republic chapter. The chapter follows the player through revolutionary printing, casualty records, a siege hospital, contested property records, Kindred feeding politics, French bombardment, and the fall of the Republic.

The chapter has four initial approaches and converges through two pressure points: the working press during bombardment and the siege itself. The final decision is not about choosing a factional victory; it asks what the player spends effort preserving when the government is clearly going to fall: people, methods, or records.

## Consequences added

Earlier eras now reach into 1849 in concrete ways:

- Renaissance refuge-rule outcomes can unlock the hospital route.
- Agnese's medieval network or plague relief network can enable a night evacuation.
- Renaissance archive poisoning or contradictory claims can be weaponized against a new confiscation order.
- Beatrice's printer mark survives as a distributed craft tradition rather than a magical bloodline.
- The old Prisca block and refuge-rule border survive as physical printing artifacts whose meaning has changed.

New state tracks casualty-record policy, hospital policy, evacuation, archive tactics, Kindred feeding restrictions, safe routes, press output, and the final people/methods/records preservation choice.

## Known weaknesses

- Chapter-to-chapter transitions remain inconsistent. Chapter 6 ends without an explicit route into `revolution_arrival`.
- The Chapter 1 ending prose still contains repeated authorial pacing language about the Embrace and needs a direct rewrite.
- The actual Embrace/sire pathway remains the largest continuity gap in the game.
- Chapter 7 currently models the siege through narrative choices rather than a resource-pressure system; that is acceptable for now but could gain mechanical teeth later.

The game now has enough historical breadth that continuity is more valuable than another isolated chapter.
