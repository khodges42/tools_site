# Scheduled Iteration Context

Work on the existing game at games/ashes-of-rome. Do not create a new game.

Read README.md, DEVELOPMENT.md, LEGAL.md, docs/loom-writing.md, story.yaml, every chapter file, and recent commits before changing anything.

This is a long-form historical interactive-fiction project built on Loom. The player begins mortal in Rome. Economic position, work, education, relationships, politics, religion, art, and behavior are established through play. The eventual clan and sire should emerge from that lived background and the player's choices rather than a direct clan-selection menu.

The long-term arc crosses multiple historical eras and reaches modern nights. Historical jumps are valuable only when they transform earlier consequences. Build durable threads that can evolve across time: people into families and institutions; favors into obligations and customs; lies into rumors and doctrine; enemies into networks and factions; objects into inheritance and disputed relics; places into institutions, ruins, and redevelopments.

Torpor is a narrative editing tool. It can make the player miss events, wake too early or late, and discover that remembered people and choices have been distorted.

Keep real history structurally important. Supernatural politics can exploit, survive, distort, and react to history, but should not become the secret cause of every human event.

Player feedback recorded in DEVELOPMENT.md outranks autonomous plans.

For each pass: diagnose before changing; choose the highest-leverage weakness; make one coherent improvement or related set of improvements; test what is testable; update DEVELOPMENT.md with changes, remaining weaknesses, and the recommended next investigation. Do not advance the timeline merely because later chapter files are empty. Deepen the current era until moving forward creates more consequences than it discards.

You may modify the local Loom engine when the story demonstrates a concrete need. Prefer small, understandable extensions. Document engine changes and preserve static hosting, mobile usability, client-side saves, and the hackable spirit of Loom.

Avoid generic AI prose, vague prophecy language, and history-textbook exposition. Use concrete people, material pressures, places, jobs, institutions, food, rent, law, worship, patronage, travel, and social obligations. The story should earn emotional stakes before demanding that the player care about centuries of consequences.

Dark horror/vampire related subject matter is allowed within normal platform safety boundaries, PG-13 equivalent movie rating, avoid sexual content in general , hateful caricature, or real historical suffering as decorative supernatural lore.
