# Ashes of Rome

A historical interactive-fiction project built with Loom. The player begins mortal in Rome and carries choices, relationships, debts, myths, and enemies across centuries.

## Project direction

- Begin mortal. Class, work, education, attachments, politics, religion, art, and survival choices establish the character through play.
- Historical jumps transform consequences rather than merely checking old flags.
- Long-lived consequence chains should evolve across people, families, institutions, myths, obligations, traditions, rumors, doctrines, and factions.
- Long sleeps are a narrative editing tool. The player can miss history, wake early or late, and encounter distorted versions of what they left behind.
- Player feedback outranks autonomous development plans.

## Chapter spine

1. Mortal Rome
2. Late Empire
3. Fall of the Western Empire
4. Medieval awakening
5. Plague era
6. Renaissance and Reformation
7. Revolution and industrialization
8. World Wars
9. Late twentieth century
10. Modern nights

Never advance the timeline merely because the next chapter is missing. Deepen the current game until advancing history creates more consequences than it discards.
