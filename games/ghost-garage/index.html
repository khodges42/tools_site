THREE YEARS AFTER THE FUNERAL

The car is still here.

Lilith has been paying rent on a garage she barely opens.

Inside: her dad's half-finished S14, a dead battery, four flat-spotted tires, and a toolbox nobody has touched since the hospital.

He always said they would get it running together. She always said she was busy.

Tonight she brings a battery charger, a gas-station coffee, and the old laptop from the bottom drawer of his toolbox.

The first job is not making power. The first job is finding out whether the thing will start.
