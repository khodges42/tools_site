Ghost Font Decoder

Load an animated GIF and watch a small motion-analysis pipeline try to recover the message. Everything runs locally in your browser. Nothing is uploaded.

Choose a GIF to begin.

What this is doing

Each tile is tested against two hypotheses: “did this patch move left?” and “did it move right?” The decoder compares many frame pairs, accumulates the winning direction for every tile, and thresholds the result. It is deliberately narrow, not a general optical-flow implementation.

Large GIFs are sampled and downscaled before analysis so the page does not keep every full-resolution frame in memory.
