Ghost Font Decoder

Load an animated GIF and watch a very small motion-analysis pipeline try to recover the message. Everything runs locally in your browser. Nothing is uploaded.

Choose a GIF to begin.

What this is doing

Each tile is tested against two small hypotheses: “did this patch move left?” and “did it move right?” The decoder compares many frame pairs, accumulates the winning direction for every tile, and then thresholds the result. It is a deliberately narrow decoder for this particular toy, not a general optical-flow implementation.

Dark and light motion tiles represent opposite horizontal directions. Mid-gray means the decoder is uncertain. If the recovered mask looks blank, try increasing the frame gap, lowering confidence, or setting motion distance manually.
